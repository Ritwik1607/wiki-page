Peg Hurley Dawson

Not to be confused with Margaret Dawson (disambiguation).

Peg Hurley Dawson is an American psychotherapist, author, and sex therapy supervisor based in North Andover, Massachusetts. She is known for her work in integrating sand therapy with trauma treatment and sexual health counseling. Dawson holds certifications as a Certified Sex Therapist Supervisor (CSTS) from the American Association of Sex Educators, Counselors, and Therapists (AASECT) and as a Registered Sandplay Practitioner (RSP).[1]

Career and Research

Dawson specializes in treating complex trauma, sexual health issues, and emotional challenges through integrated therapeutic approaches. She developed several innovative treatment methods, including Discovery Sandtray Therapy and Pro-Symptom Belief Cards (PSBC). Her work combines traditional psychotherapy with creative modalities, particularly emphasizing the role of sand therapy in treating trauma and behavioral issues.[2]

She authored "Sand Therapy for Out of Control Sexual Behavior, Shame, and Trauma: Treatment Approaches Beyond Words" (Routledge, 2023), a clinical textbook exploring the intersection of sand therapy and sexual health treatment.[3]

Therapeutic Approaches

Sand Therapy Methods

Dawson is recognized for her expertise in various sand therapy techniques:

  • Kalffian Sandplay Therapy: A Jungian psychology-based approach focusing on symbolic representation
  • Discovery Sandtray Therapy: An integrated method combining Kalffian Sandplay, Internal Family Systems, somatic therapies, and EMDR
  • SandStory Therapy: A narrative-focused approach developed by Lara Kasza

Additional Modalities

Dawson employs multiple evidence-based therapeutic approaches:[4]

  • Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS)
  • Polyvagal Theory applications
  • Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP)

Clinical Specializations

Her clinical practice addresses:

  • Complex trauma and PTSD
  • Sexual health and behavior
  • Attachment and relationship issues
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Medically unexplained symptoms (MUS)

Innovations

Dawson developed the Pro-Symptom Belief Cards (PSBC), a therapeutic tool designed to help clients identify and address negative core beliefs often manifesting as Imposter Syndrome or self-doubt. These beliefs are typically linked to early childhood experiences stored in the brain's default mode network.[5]
